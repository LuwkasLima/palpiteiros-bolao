---
description: Keep changes simple and contained
---

- Prefer the smallest correct change that resolves the problem.
- Avoid unrelated refactors unless they are necessary for correctness.
- When a fix feels hacky, consider whether a cleaner solution is practical within the current scope.
- Optimize for readability, maintainability, and low regression risk.
- If a change is large or complex enough to require significant explanation, consider breaking it into smaller, more focused pieces.
